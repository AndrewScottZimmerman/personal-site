## About
### How I got into Financial Services
Like most high-schoolers, I started college expecting to do something else. At the time, I was convinced that Computer Science was the career path for me. My thinking changed when I realized how deeply economics affects everything: how people connect through transactions, how systems behave and adjust in unexpected ways. The realization that I could use my understanding to help people reach their goals, such as buying a home, paying for their children’s education, or saving for retirement, pulled me into investments and has kept me there.

### What I do at Fidelity Investments
I work as a Wealth Management Service Specialist at Fidelity Investments, holding the Series 7 and Series 63 licenses. In this role, I assist advisors and clients with Transfer of Assets requests, account maintenance, and monetary transactions, acting as a case manager to ensure each request is completed promptly and to a high standard. The role sits between operations and service, where I work to bridge the gap and make things easier for everyone involved.

### Why Do I Build Software?
I started developing code in fifth grade and eventually published my first app on the App Store at the age of fifteen. Finance came later, with age, and the two interests naturally converged as I instinctively wanted to blend them. Software skills are now vital in investment work, and building my own tools enables me to grow both skills and provides concrete examples to potential employers.

### What Position Am I Looking For?
I am primarily looking at roles in Advisement or Analysis, ideally at the intersection of both, such as an Investment Management Consultant role. A position like this appeals to me because of the combination of understanding the technical complexities of investments while also serving as a steady and reassuring voice to clients whose wealth depends on it.

### Getting Personal
When I’m not working, I can be found dirt biking, snow skiing, water skiing, playing the occasional video game, or running my D&D campaign with my friends. When I’m on vacation, you can almost certainly find me hiding behind a camera as I capture landscapes to enjoy. If you’re interested in my favorite latest read, it’s Atomic Habits by James Clear.
